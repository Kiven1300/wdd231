Discover Xochimilco

Xochimilco is one of the sixteen boroughs of Mexico City and one of its most beloved destinations. Declared a UNESCO World Heritage Site in 1987, it is home to the ancient chinampa agricultural system, colorful trajineras, vibrant markets, and a rich indigenous heritage that stretches back thousands of years.

Top Attractions

Trajinera Canal Tours

Glide through the ancient canal system on a colorful flat-bottomed boat called a trajinera. Musicians, food vendors, and flower sellers paddle alongside — an experience unlike anywhere else in the world.

Mercado de Xochimilco

One of the most traditional markets in Mexico City, offering fresh produce, flowers, handcrafts, street food, and goods sourced directly from local chinampa farms.

Chinampas (Floating Gardens)

The ancient Aztec agricultural technique of building fertile islands on shallow lake beds. These living plots still produce flowers, vegetables, and herbs for Mexico City markets today.

Parroquia de San Bernardino de Siena

A stunning 16th-century Franciscan church and monastery at the heart of the historic center, featuring a richly decorated altarpiece and beautiful colonial architecture.

Isla de las Muñecas

The famous "Island of the Dolls" — a unique and mysterious chinampa covered in hundreds of old dolls, originally hung by caretaker Julián Santana Barrera. Now a popular cultural landmark.

Flower Festival (Feria de la Flor)

Held annually, this vibrant celebration showcases the flower-growing tradition of Xochimilco with floral arrangements, competitions, music, and artisan markets.

Local Economy

Xochimilco's economy is deeply rooted in agriculture, tourism, and small commerce. The chinampa system supports hundreds of family-run farms producing flowers and vegetables. Tourism generated by the canal network supports trajinera operators, restaurants, artisans, and vendors year-round.

The Chamber of Commerce works to connect these businesses, promote sustainable practices, and attract new investment that respects the cultural and ecological heritage of the area.
